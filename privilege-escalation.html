What is Privilege Escalation?

Privilege escalation is an attack that by taking advantage of a vulnerability (see password attacks, binary exploitation, xss for examples of vulnerabilities) in a program/app the attacker can gain elevated privilege and access resources that are typically restricted to them.

Attackers can exploit privilege escalation vulnerabilities to do the following, accessing sensitive data, modifying configurations, or executing privileged commands.

Two Types of Privilege Escalation

Vertical Privilege Escalation: When an attacker gains higher level privileges, like moving from a regular user to an admin user.

Horizontal Privilege Escalation: When an attacker gains another user's privileges without gaining elevated access, such as impersonating another standard user, who might be in a better position to apply vertical privilege escalation.

Mitigation Techniques

Common ways to reduce the risk of privilege escalation include:

  • Using strict access controls and the principle of least privilege.
  • Making sure to regularly update and patch software to fix known vulnerabilities.
  • Requiring multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Monitoring and auditing logs to detect and respond to suspicious activity. Often predefined actions that shouldn't be taken are logged and sent to other admins
