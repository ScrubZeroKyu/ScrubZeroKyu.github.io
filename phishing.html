What is Phishing?

Phishing is a common type of social engineering attack where attackers pretend to be someone else in order to trick individuals into providing sensitive information, such as login credentials, credit card details, IDs etc.

Example: One of the most common phishing tactic is using e-mails where they pretend to be someone else such as your bank provider.

Prevention: Avoid clicking on links from e-mails or other insecure applications and instead find it on the actual page where possible, verify the sender's email address, or use virtual machines to check if the link is leads to malware. If you click on a link and the page looks okay check the SSL certificate of the page.

What is Spear Phishing?

Spear phishing is a targeted version of phishing usually aimed at specific people or organisations. Unlike generic phishing attacks, spear phishing attacks are personalised, making them more convincing and harder to detect.

Example: An attacker may research a specific employee in an organisation and create a specific email that appears to come from a known colleague, requesting sensitive information, often time with emotional language or time limits to put the user into a hotstate.

Prevention: Be suspicious of random requests for sensitive information, verify where you can, and ask for more personal information that a third party wouldn't know.
